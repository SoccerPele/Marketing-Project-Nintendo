Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about the eShop Game Finder — how it works, how your data is used, and how to get the best results.

About the Feature

The eShop Game Finder is a new personalized recommendation feature built directly into Nintendo's eShop. It combines a short three-question quiz about your gaming preferences with your existing purchase history to surface Switch games you're most likely to enjoy.

Think of it as a smart assistant for the eShop — instead of browsing thousands of titles, you get a curated shortlist tailored specifically to you.

The current eShop shows general "recommended for you" sections based on sales trends and broad categories. The Game Finder goes further by capturing your active preferences through the quiz — your taste for intensity, social play, and storytelling — and blending that with your personal library history.

The result is a recommendation that understands not just what you've bought, but what you actually want to play next.

No! You can take the quiz and see recommendations without signing in. However, if you're logged into your Nintendo Account, the engine can also factor in your purchase history for even more accurate picks. Your profile page will reflect your saved results and library data when signed in.

Research shows that users drop off sharply when quizzes exceed 3–5 questions. Our three questions — relaxation level, social preference, and story importance — are the highest-signal dimensions for predicting game satisfaction on Switch. They map to distinct genres cleanly and provide enough signal without friction.

We pair them with purchase history (which carries the rest of the weight) to keep the quiz fast while maintaining recommendation quality.

How Recommendations Work

Each game in our catalog is scored on the same three dimensions as the quiz (relaxation, social play, narrative depth). When you submit your answers, we calculate the "distance" between your preferences and every game's profile — the closest match becomes your top pick.

When purchase history is available, we boost games that are genre-adjacent to your library and reduce suggestions for games you already own.

Yes! Your mood and gaming goals change — that's expected. The quiz is designed to be retaken anytime. If you're in the mood for something new after finishing a long RPG, shift your sliders and the engine will surface something fitting. Past results are saved in your profile so you can compare them over time.

When two games are equally close to your profile, we use secondary signals to break the tie: your purchase history for genre affinity, review scores, and recency of the title. Ties are rare because all three quiz dimensions must match equally across multiple axis simultaneously. Both tied games typically appear in your "you might also like" section.

Privacy & Data

The quiz answers (three numeric values, 1–5 each) are processed locally in your browser and are never sent to Nintendo's servers unless you're signed into a Nintendo Account and have opted in to personalization features.

If signed in, the feature accesses your eShop purchase history — the same data already stored with your Nintendo Account — to improve recommendations. No new data is collected; existing data is used in a new way.

Absolutely. You can opt out at any time via Nintendo Account → Privacy Settings → Personalized Recommendations. When opted out, the Game Finder still works — it uses only your quiz answers, not your purchase history. You'll still receive useful recommendations, just without the library-aware boost.

No. Quiz answers and recommendation history are used solely to improve your own experience within Nintendo's ecosystem. They are never sold to or shared with third-party advertisers or publishers. Please review Nintendo's full Privacy Policy for comprehensive details on all data handling.

Technical

The Game Finder is available on the Nintendo Switch eShop (all hardware variants including Switch, Switch Lite, and Switch OLED), the Nintendo eShop web portal, and the Nintendo Switch mobile app for iOS and Android. Results sync across all platforms when you're signed into your Nintendo Account.

A game won't appear if: (a) you already own it, (b) it's been filtered out due to regional availability, (c) it's not yet part of our recommendation catalog (new releases take 2–4 weeks to be scored), or (d) it simply wasn't the closest match to your profile. Try adjusting your quiz answers — sometimes a small shift reveals different suggestions.

The recommendation catalog is updated weekly. New releases are reviewed and scored by our team within 2–4 weeks of launch. Major first-party Nintendo titles are fast-tracked and typically appear within days of release. You can always check the "New This Week" section on the main eShop for the very latest additions.
